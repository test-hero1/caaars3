A comprehensive history of residential real estate

In 2019, there are more than 100 countries where new housing construction is taking place. And this is only in the developed world. And this is only in the South-East Asia region, and in some places in the world — in Africa, East Asia, the Middle East and the North. In 2019, more than 100 countries and territories are expected to see significant growth in the number of isolated housing units (IHVUs), and the number of IHVUs in the first half of 2020 will exceed 200 000.As a result, the construction of 100% foreign investment in isolated housing is unlikely to continue for a long time. And even if the construction of 150,000 homes, the number of IHVUs is still not quite there. According to the developers themselves, the supply chain is complex and it is likely that many companies will not adequately prepare for and respond to this demand. But the trend is encouraging.At the moment, the most actively investing in IHVUs is from Asia. In 2018, more than 1.4 million IHVUs were built in this direction. In 2019, more than 1.6 million IHVUs were built in this direction. And by 2023, this number will decrease to about 400 000 homes.Developers hope that IHS (International Homes, Interiors) will replace IHVUs because of the high efficiency of installation and manufacturing of structures made of modern industrial processes. Moreover, the production of inexpensive structures can be significantly cheaper.Developers believe that the demand for IHS (Industrial Information) will grow exponentially as firms with the ability to quickly produce and distribute capital increase their production capacity.
